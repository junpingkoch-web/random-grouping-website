How to Use

  1. Paste your list: add everyone's name to the box above, one per line, or separated by commas.
  2. Pick a split method: "By number of groups" fits a fixed group count, like splitting into 4 teams; "By people per group" fits a fixed group size, like 5 people per group.
  3. Set the number: enter the group count or group size — the tool balances everyone automatically, so group sizes never differ by more than one person.
  4. Click "Shuffle & Group": the list is randomly shuffled and dealt evenly into groups, shown instantly below with a color marker per group.
  5. Not happy with the draw? click "Reshuffle" to re-draw the same list, or "Copy Results" to paste the outcome into a doc or chat.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is the grouping truly random, or could it favor certain people?

Yes, it's truly random. The tool first shuffles the entire list with the Fisher-Yates algorithm, then deals names into groups in order, so every person has an equal chance of landing in any group — there's no way to bias the outcome.

What happens if the group size doesn't divide evenly?

The tool automatically works out how many groups are needed and spreads any remainder evenly across them, so group sizes never differ by more than one person.

What happens with duplicate names in my list?

Turn on "Automatically remove duplicate names" to strip exact duplicates before grouping. Leave it off to keep duplicates — useful if you're distinguishing same-named people with IDs or numbers.

How many people can this tool handle at once?

Everything runs locally in your browser, so there's no hard limit — lists from a handful of people to several hundred shuffle instantly. For very large lists, splitting by people-per-group keeps each group a manageable size.

Is my list uploaded or stored anywhere?

No. Every name and every result is computed and displayed only in your own browser — nothing is uploaded or stored on any server, and everything clears when you refresh the page.
